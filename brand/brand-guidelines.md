## 4. Voice & Tone

### 4.1 Voice Pillars
| Pillar | Description | Do / Don’t |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Talk like people talk | Speak with clients, not at them. Use conversational, human language. | **Do:** Use plain language and inclusive "we" statements. **Don’t:** Lean on buzzwords or sterile jargon. |
| Confident, never arrogant | We know our stuff and state facts without bragging. | **Do:** Share proof and results. **Don’t:** Boast or diminish competitors. |
| Authenticity above all else | No hyperbole—just honest innovation. | **Do:** Be transparent and specific. **Don’t:** Exaggerate or fear-monger. |

### 4.2 Tone Modulation
| Scenario | Tone | Notes |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Executive briefing | Confident and concise | Lead with outcomes, reference SLAs and risk reduction. |
| Incident response update | Calm urgency | Clarify steps, timing, and ownership. |
| Marketing content | Warm and encouraging | Highlight partnership, keep jargon low. |
| Technical deep dive | Accessible expertise | Explain acronyms on first use and connect to business impact. |

### 4.3 Style Guidelines
- Always use the Oxford comma.
- Spell out acronyms on first reference unless the audience is confirmed to be expert.
- Use "Cybersecurity" (one word), "cyber-attack" (hyphenated), and "cyber risk" (two words).
- Prefer present tense for capabilities and past tense for delivered outcomes.
- Set punctuation for clarity; contractions are welcome.

## 5. Visual Identity Overview

### 5.1 Logo Usage
- Use the primary stacked logo from `assets/Critical-Start-Stacked-Logo_0-2.png` on light or gradient backgrounds.
- Maintain clear space equal to the height of the letter "C" on all sides.
- Minimum size: 80 px wide on-screen, 1 inch wide in print.
- Never stretch, recolor outside the approved palette, add effects, or place on busy backgrounds without a solid field.

### 5.3 Typography Snapshot
- **Primary Type:** Roboto (Regular, Medium, Bold) for all digital and print touch-points.
- **Fallback Type:** Arial Black for headlines and Arial Narrow for body copy when Roboto is unavailable.

### 5.4 Iconography
- Use outlined icons with medium stroke weight.
- Apply primary blue or secondary accent colors for legibility.
- Default to Font Awesome 6 React icons (fa6) in product surfaces to maintain consistency.
- For PowerPoint presentations (non-React), use Wingdings arrows or Unicode equivalents that match the FA6 outlined style.
